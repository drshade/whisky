# Compass Box Orangerie — 2026-05-30

_Sampled at dinner with friends. The menu listed it as "Oak Cross & Orangerie" — those are
two separate Compass Box bottlings; the **very citrusy** one I tasted is the **Orangerie**._

## Verdict
Very **citrusy** — plus vanilla, and obviously whisky underneath. Distinctive and easy.

## What it actually is
Not a normal Scotch: Glen Moray malt + Cameronbridge grain whisky **infused with hand-zested
Navelina orange peel, cassia bark and cloves.** That's why the orange is so forward — it's
closer to a flavoured whisky / aperitif than a traditional dram.

## Overall
Fun and novel, but the infusion does the heavy lifting rather than cask complexity — so
probably more of a curiosity than a profile fit for me (I chase *whisky* complexity). Worth
having tried; not chasing a bottle.
