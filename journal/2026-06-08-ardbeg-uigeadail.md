# Ardbeg Uigeadail — 2026-06-08

Opened after the Bain's 15yr — a deliberate leap to the other end of the spectrum.

## Nose
- Obviously peated with a medicinal bite
- Fresh banana leaves
- Grassy, fresh-cut sweetness cutting through the smoke
- Opened up with air — sherry started pushing forward, smoke integrating

## Palate
- Toffee, candy, liquorice
- Roasted sweet potato — the signature smoke-meets-sherry note
- Smoky but mild and medicinal rather than aggressive
- Sherry doing exactly what it should — sweetness leading, smoke supporting

## Finish
- Shortish and unremarkable — but happily so
- Heat drops cleanly, doesn't linger
- Lets you look forward to the next sip rather than overstaying its welcome

## Overall
The Uigeadail paradox in full effect — peat and medicinal bite on the nose, then sherry
takes over on the palate and the smoke steps back. Wonderful complexity, especially that
roasted sweet potato note which feels unique to this expression. The finish is the one
weak point but it earns its place by not getting in the way. A genuinely great whisky.
