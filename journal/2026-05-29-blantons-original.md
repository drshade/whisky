# Blanton's Original — 2026-05-29

_Reflective note comparing my bourbons. See also [Gold Edition](2026-05-29-blantons-gold-edition.md)._

## Verdict
The **nicest of my bourbons**. Most caramel and sweet of the lot — very delicious. The
balance at 46.5% feels just right: rich and sweet without any harshness getting in the way.

Current favourite bourbon on the shelf. Hits the sweet-and-rich note I clearly enjoy.

## Revisit note (2026-05-29)
This was one of my **first bourbons** — it opened my eyes to what bourbon could be, so the
**89 may carry first-love / novelty bias.** I haven't re-tasted it since my palate
developed, and the later, more-considered bourbons (Baby Jane, Blanton's Gold) landed
*disappointingly* — a hint my bar has risen. Direct parallel to the Dalwhinnie, which slid
from "loved" to "dull" once I had better references.

Honestly nervous to revisit — don't want to spoil the original joy of discovering it. But
the memory of that first moment is mine to keep regardless of how it tastes now. **Plan:**
re-taste fairly, side-by-side with a more complex bourbon (e.g. Larceny BP), before fully
trusting the 89. Until then, treat 89 as *sentimental / unverified*.
