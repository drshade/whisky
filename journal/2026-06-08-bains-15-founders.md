# Bain's Cape Mountain 15yr Founders Collection — 2026-06-08

Bought on impulse at OR Tambo duty free the day before (R1,255) — saw it, knew it was a
limited run of 3,600 bottles only available at two airports and the distillery, bought it
immediately. Opened it at CT airport the following evening.

## Nose
- Initially fiery but not unpleasant
- Less sweet than the standard Bain's — peppery and tart
- Earth and charcoal
- Tiniest hint of smoke with a sweeter edge
- Almost unripe green apple
- Faint sunscreen / lotion note (oak lactones)
- With water: liquorice emerges, and banana comes through clearly

## Palate
- The higher ABV is present up front — wood spice and tannin lead
- Fruit sits underneath; less sweet than the standard but more structured
- The complexity the standard never had

## Finish
- The heat disappears quickly — no burn lingering
- Leaves a pleasant brightness, mild and tingly
- Longer and cleaner than the standard

## Overall
Meaningfully better than the standard Bain's (78) — more complex, more to explore, and
the water trick opened it up nicely. The higher ABV and wood spice mean it demands
attention rather than casual sipping, so not as all-day drinkable as the standard. But
for a deliberate pour it's a lovely whisky and a great find at the airport. Would
absolutely buy again if a friend can get their hands on one — stocks won't last forever.
