# 🥃 Tasting Room Cheat Sheet

Pull this up at the NPF Tasting Room bar. Goal: **explore styles I don't own**, then
buy the winners (R1000–2000) at the NGF bottle store.

## Opening line
> "I'm fairly new and deliberately exploring — so far I've loved everything: peated &
> unpeated, cask strength & 43%, bourbon & sherried Scotch. **Can you build me a flight
> that takes me somewhere new?**"

## What I already own (so don't pour these)
- **Peat / high ABV:** Kilchoman Sanaig Cask Strength (Islay, 57.8%)
- **Highland malt:** Oban 14, Dalwhinnie 15, Glenmorangie Duthac
- **Kentucky bourbon:** Blanton's (×2), Buffalo Trace, Woodford Double Oaked, Widow Jane Baby Jane
- **Grain / blended malt:** Compass Box Hedonism & Crimson Casks

## Styles I haven't tried — pour me these
- **Rye** (spicy contrast to bourbon)
- **Irish single pot still** (creamy/oily — e.g. Redbreast)
- **Campbeltown** (briny/funky — Springbank, Kilkerran)
- **Japanese** (the pricey/allocated stuff especially)
- **World whisky** — India (Amrut), Taiwan (Kavalan), Australia (Starward)

## Three asks that get the most from the host
1. "What's something I almost certainly haven't tried that you think I'd love?"
2. "Anything rare or allocated that's open right now?" *(this is the point of 15ml pours)*
3. "If I love one of these, which is actually buyable — and is it under R2000?"

## Budget
- **Buy:** up to **R2000/bottle**. → "Flag which of these I can take home in that range."
- **Just taste:** aged Japanese single malts, allocated Springbank, single-cask Kavalan
  (usually >R2000 at retail — the bar is the smart way to try them).

## Don't
- Don't say "I like smooth/smoky" and stop — it boxes the host in.
- "Surprise me, here's what I haven't had" → far better flight in discovery mode.
