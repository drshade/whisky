# Recommendations — Regions & Styles to Explore

A curated map of the territory you *haven't* covered yet, built for **variety/discovery
mode**. Each section has solid, well-regarded picks across three tiers:

- **Entry** — affordable, easy to find, a faithful intro to the style.
- **Benchmark** — the bottle the style is judged against; buy with confidence.
- **Splurge** — worth it when you want to go deeper (often scarcer/pricier).

Find-ability is for ZA (Norman Goodfellows / The Tasting Room, Melrose):
🟢 widely stocked · 🟡 around if you look · 🔴 scarce / allocated.

> Prices are rough ZA ballparks — confirm on the shelf. Once you buy one, move it to
> `collection.md`; if you want to try it, add it to `wishlist.md`.

## 2. Irish — single pot still  *(a whole style you've not touched)*
Creamy, spicy, oily texture unique to Ireland. The pot-still category is the one to chase.

| Bottle | ABV | Note | Tier | Find |
|--------|-----|------|------|------|
| Jameson Black Barrel | 40% | Easy, rich entry into the Irish idea | Entry | 🟢 |
| **Redbreast 12** | 40% | The pot-still benchmark — sherried, lush, a must-try | Benchmark | 🟢 |
| Green Spot | 40% | Lighter, orchard-fruit pot still; great everyday | Benchmark | 🟡 |
| Redbreast 15 / Powers John's Lane 12 | 46% | Deeper, oilier, full-strength pot still | Splurge | 🟡 |

## 7. Wheated bourbon  *(you have rye-mash bourbons — wheat is the softer cousin)*
Wheat replaces rye in the mash → softer, sweeter, rounder. Different bourbon dialect.

| Bottle | ABV | Note | Tier | Find |
|--------|-----|------|------|------|

## Suggested first moves
If I were prioritising for *maximum new ground* per Rand:
1. **Redbreast 12** — opens up Irish pot still (🟢, ~R600).
2. **Nikka From The Barrel** — opens up Japan, huge value (🟡, ~R750).
3. **Rittenhouse Rye BiB** — opens up rye (🟡, ~R500).
4. **Amrut Fusion** — opens up world whisky (🟡, ~R700).

Four bottles, four entirely new style-worlds. Say the word and I'll add any of these to `wishlist.md`.
