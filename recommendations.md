# Recommendations — Regions & Styles to Explore

A curated map of the territory you *haven't* covered yet, built for **variety/discovery
mode**. Each section has solid, well-regarded picks across three tiers:

- **Entry** — affordable, easy to find, a faithful intro to the style.
- **Benchmark** — the bottle the style is judged against; buy with confidence.
- **Splurge** — worth it when you want to go deeper (often scarcer/pricier).

Find-ability is for your **home market, the Netherlands / EU** (Gall & Gall for mainstream;
whisky.nl / Drankdozijn and EU/UK specialists like Master of Malt & The Whisky Exchange for
the rest):
🟢 widely stocked · 🟡 specialist shops / order online · 🔴 scarce, allocated, or flips above RRP.

> Prices are rough **EU retail** ballparks in € — confirm on the shelf. Once you buy one,
> move it to `collection.md`; if you want to try it, add it to `wishlist.md`.
>
> **Buying budget: €50–100 per bottle (R1000–2000 on SA trips).** Covers every Benchmark pick
> here and most Splurges. The main things it *won't* reach at retail are aged Japanese single
> malts, allocated American whiskey (Weller, Stagg, GTS) and allocated Springbank — taste
> those at a bar / the NPF Tasting Room instead of buying blind.
>
> **NL/EU vs SA:** Scotch, Irish, Japanese blends and world whisky are broadly available here;
> American whiskey is *easier and cheaper* at EU retail than in SA (barrel-proof Wild Turkey,
> Rittenhouse), but US allocated bottles stay scarce everywhere. Save SA trips for *local* SA
> whisky (Bain's, Three Ships).
